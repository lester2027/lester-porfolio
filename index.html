How I got into tech

My interest in computers started when I was young. I spent a lot of time playing computer games and exploring anything related to technology. I was always curious about how things worked and enjoyed creating and designing things on the computer.

However, my career path didn’t immediately lead me into technology. I graduated with a degree in Psychology and later became a teacher. Teaching was a meaningful experience, but over time I realized that my curiosity for technology and building things had never really disappeared.

While teaching, I started learning web development in my spare time. What began as simple curiosity quickly turned into a passion. I found myself enjoying the process of writing code, solving problems, and designing interfaces that bring ideas to life.

Eventually, I made the decision to step away from teaching and fully pursue my passion for technology. Since then, I’ve been focused on learning web and software development, building projects, and improving my skills every day as I work toward becoming a full-stack developer.

For me, coding is more than just writing programs — it’s about creating, solving problems, and continuously learning something new.
